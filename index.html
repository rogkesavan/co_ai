Mission

Coral reefs cover up to 0.5% of the ocean floor to protect 30% of worlds' marine fish species and other 400 million people who rely on them for work, food, and protection. Millions of images are taken using satellite imagery, AUV and GPS but the process of analysing it is a tedious work for marine export as it takes 10-30 min at least for a single image. Researchers feel very difficult to analyse all set of images and draw an analysis report on their life cycle which makes a situation that they are unable to recover the affected region and preserve the enrich part.

This project aims to bring up an image classification and segmentation module which uses deep learning models, drone and satellite images to find their level of growth, calculate their disappearing rate, identify the affected zone and count of existing corals so that efficient methods can be taken to resolve the decreasing rates. These techniques can also be used to identify nutrient required parts of the baby corals. This model will be integrated with an underwater drone software for real time prediction and analysing software which helps the researcher to get the report in ease.
